cleaner taste.

Glass doesn't burn, doesn't flake, and doesn't absorb flavor. Every pull comes through cleaner than paper or cardboard — the way the flower was supposed to hit in the first place.

— Inquiries

The common
questions.

i.

How do I clean the tips?

Warm water handles most of it. For stubborn buildup, drop them in isopropyl alcohol for an hour, rinse, and they're back to factory clear. Dishwasher-safe on the top rack, if you're feeling lazy.

ii.

Are these legal to buy?

Yes. Sold as reusable glass rolling tips for legal herb and tobacco use. Same category as any other tip or filter you'd find in a shop — no license, no ID, no special paperwork.

iii.

How many times can I reuse them?

Hundreds. The glass itself doesn't wear out — it only breaks if you drop it. One tip replaces thousands of cardboard crutches over its useful life.

iv.

What size joints are they best for?

Designed for fat rolls — anywhere from a king-size up to whatever you feel like packing. Standard joints also fit fine, you'll just have a slightly roomier draw.

v.

How long does shipping take?

Every piece ships tracked and insured within one to two business days. Most US orders arrive within three to five days after that. Grail pieces ship white-glove, double-boxed, signature-required.

vi.

Why does a glass tip cost this much?

A five-dollar glass tip is machine-pulled by the thousand in a factory you've never heard of. These are hand-blown by real glassworkers, then hitoki personally checks each one before it goes on the bench. You're paying for the hand that made it, the eye that approved it, and a piece that holds its value instead of ending up in a landfill.
