For the work that needs more than a website.

Shipside works on the systems behind the business — intake, follow-up, scheduling, CRM cleanup, workflow, and the practical AI that earns its keep. The website is the first impression. The systems are how the work actually runs.

What this work is

Most of the businesses we talk to are running fine on referrals and the work itself. What slips is the connective tissue: a missed call no one circles back on, a scheduling tangle that lives in three places, a follow-up that depends on someone remembering. We build the small system that closes those gaps and stays out of the way.

Intake and follow-up

Where leads, calls, quote requests, and replies actually land. One list, one place, with reminders that fire when something hasn't been answered.

Scheduling

Booking flows, route windows, and confirmation/reminder texts that match how the crew actually works — not a generic calendar widget.

Light CRM

A clean record of who's a lead, who's a customer, what was quoted, what was won, and what needs a check-in. Owner-readable, no consultant required to use it.

Workflow cleanup

The manual stitching between email, texts, spreadsheets, and software. We map it, find what's eating time, and rebuild the part that pays back fastest.

Practical AI

AI where it earns its keep — call summaries, intake triage, draft replies, document parsing, simple internal tools. Not the hype kind. The kind that quietly takes work off the plate.

Internal tools

Custom dashboards, lookups, and small admin apps so the team doesn't paste data between tabs all day.

When this fits

The website is one piece of a bigger problem.

The site is fine, or about to be. The drag is somewhere else — leads dropping, scheduling friction, or a workflow held together with notes and good memory.

Manual work is slowing the team down.

Someone is doing the same task by hand every day, or the day starts with checking three inboxes to see what came in overnight.

The current tools don't fit how you actually operate.

You bought the platform, the team works around it, and the real workflow lives in messages and spreadsheets.

You need a partner to think it through and build it.

You can feel something is off but turning that into a clear next system is the part that hasn't happened. We help map it, scope it, and build the smallest useful piece first.

How Shipside approaches it

01
Understand the workflow.

Short conversation, then a closer look at how the work actually moves through the business today — not how the org chart says it does.

02
Find the real constraint.

The thing that, if it stopped breaking, would unblock the most other things. We name it in writing before any build work starts.

03
Design the smallest useful system.

The version that fixes the constraint without adding three more tools to maintain. Plain pieces, owner-readable, hand-off documented.

04
Build and test against real use.

Ship it into the actual workflow with the actual team. Watch where it bends. Adjust until it holds up on a normal week.

05
Improve from there.

Once the first piece is steady, the next constraint becomes obvious. We work the list one piece at a time. No big-bang rollouts.
