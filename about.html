01 / WHAT WE DO

What Shipside does

Shipside builds the public face and the operating layer behind it — owned websites, capture and follow-up wiring, owner-only inboxes, internal tools, and practical AI where it earns its place. Built to be used, handed off, hosted, changed, and understood. Not built to be a platform you can never leave.

02 / HOUSE RULES

How we think about the work

Fast, not fancy

The site loads fast and shows the right info. The system does its job and stays out of the way. That's the bar.

You own it

Code, domain, content, and any internal tools we build are yours. No proprietary builder. No subscription trap. Walk away any day; nothing goes dark.

Clear scope, written first

What gets built, what's included, the timing — all in writing before any deposit. No hidden meter, no scope creep dressed up as discovery.

Smallest useful piece first

Whether it's a five-page site or an internal tool, the first version fixes the real constraint and earns the next one. No big-bang rollouts.

Plain files, plain language

Static where static fits. No plugin chains to babysit. Everything we hand off is something the business can read and run.

Practical AI, not the hype kind

AI lives in the parts where it earns its keep — call summaries, intake triage, draft replies, internal lookups. Not where it adds risk for show.

03 / WHY THIS MODEL

Why this model works

Most working businesses can't stomach the bloat of a big agency or a stack of platforms that don't fit how the work actually runs. So they wait — and keep losing time to manual stitching, missed leads, and tools that almost fit.

Shipside builds the small, useful piece. A clean owned website. A real capture and follow-up layer. The internal tool that takes a half-day off the week. Plain language, owner-readable, scope locked, hand-off documented. We stay accountable end to end so there's no vendor finger-pointing when something needs a fix.

04 / ACCOUNTABILITY

Built by an operator, not a handoff chain.

One operator owns the build end to end — fit call, scope, copy, layout, capture wiring, owner-inbox setup, hand-off. No account manager passing your work to a junior. No agency sub-contracting the live build to someone you'll never meet. The person on the fit call is the person on the keyboard.

That doesn't mean lone-wolf craft. The work runs through a serious system: locked scope written first, layered architecture (L01 Owned Site → L02 Capture → L03 Owner Control → L04 Ongoing Care), plain static files, a hand-off checklist for every layer. The system is what keeps the work tight when one person is accountable.
