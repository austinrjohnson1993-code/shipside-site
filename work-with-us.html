01 / WHO IT FITS

Who Shipside is built for.

Owner-operators

Service businesses, trades, professional services, boutique product businesses. One person or a small crew running it. The operator is who we'll be wiring the system around.

Word-of-mouth driven

Most of the work comes from referrals or repeat. The website is a wedge that has to hold up to a real search-and-check, not a marketing-led top of funnel.

Workflow pain

You can name the part of the day or week that's eating time. Missed calls, scattered intake, manual booking back-and-forth, a CRM nobody opens. That's where most of our leverage shows up.

Ready to own it

Files yours, domain yours, logins yours. You want a partner, not a vendor that holds work hostage. If we ever need to walk away from each other, the build keeps running.

02 / WHAT THE WORKFLOW LOOKS LIKE

Fit call · map · scope · build · care.

Start: a fifteen-minute fit call. We tell you straight whether Shipside fits. If yes, we sketch a systems map — what runs the business now, where time gets lost, which service groups apply. Then a written scope and cost before any deposit.

Build: one team accountable end-to-end. The fit call is with the founder, the build is run by the founder, the handoff is with the founder. No baton passes.

Care: hosting handled, basic checks running, small updates and tuning on a cadence the business sets. Cancel any month — the build keeps running. You own the files.

03 / WHEN WE PASS

The honest cases where we say no.

Marketing-only scope

If the actual ask is paid ads, SEO, or top-of-funnel marketing, that's not Shipside. We build the systems behind the marketing layer. We can recommend operators we trust if that's the real need.

The leverage isn't there

Sometimes the right answer is one workflow tweak, not a system. We say so on the call instead of writing a proposal that won't change the day.
