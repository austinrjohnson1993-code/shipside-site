SHIPSIDE HOME FIELD NOTES FAQ INQUIRIES
INTEL

Questions I get asked before the first email.

Who is behind Shipside?
One person. I've worked in home services and tech. I build websites, not decks about websites. Georgetown/Austin TX based.
Why is it so cheap?
No office, no team, no account managers, no project managers. I build the thing myself. The price reflects the actual cost of the work, not the overhead of an agency.
What's the turnaround?
Teardowns ship in 48 hours. Rebuilds ship in 7 days. MVPs ship in 14 days. All from deposit date.
What tech do you use?
Next.js, Vercel, Supabase when needed. No WordPress. No page builders. No plugin stacks. Everything deploys as static HTML or edge-rendered. Sites score 90+ on Google PageSpeed.
Do you do SEO?
I set up the technical foundation — fast load, mobile-first, proper schema, GBP alignment. I don't do ongoing SEO retainers, keyword campaigns, or monthly reporting. The site ships fast and clean; ranking follows.
What about design?
I build clean, functional layouts. No custom illustration, no brand identity work, no logo design. If you need a creative agency, I'll tell you. If you need a site that converts, that's me.
What happens after the site ships?
You own it. Code lives in a GitHub repo. Hosted on Vercel's free tier (no monthly hosting fee for most sites). If you need changes later, email me — small edits are usually free, bigger changes get a flat quote.
What if I don't like it?
One revision round is included in the Rebuild. Two rounds in the MVP. If we genuinely can't align after that, I'll refund the build cost minus the teardown fee ($149). I'd rather lose a job than ship something I'm not proud of.
Can you do e-commerce?
Basic product pages, yes. Full Shopify-style cart with inventory management, no. If you're selling 3-10 products DTC, I can build that. If you need a catalog of 500 SKUs, you need Shopify.
Do you work with businesses outside Austin?
Yes. Everything is remote. I focus on local service businesses because that's where the conversion leaks are worst, but the geographic reach doesn't matter. Email works.
Still have a question?
Ship me an email.
